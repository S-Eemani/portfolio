Internet of Things security: Denial of Service defense model (Research)

In my junior year, I was accepted by the prestigious MITACS Global link program, which is conducted jointly by the governments of India and Canada. As one of the two undergraduates selected from my university, I went to Polytechnique Montreal. There I worked on a security- based project titled "Internet of Things security: Denial of Service defense model", under the supervision of Prof. Martin Bellaiche.

Our goal was to build an efficient system that blocks adversaries with high accuracy in resource-constrained environments. I used the Decision tree classification algorithm and Random Forest algorithm to choose meaningful features from the available data. It has been a pleasure to participate in lectures of such high quality, reading and discussing numerous papers and applying them at the same time. I was fortunate enough to meet people from different parts of the world during my time in Montreal. I experienced cultural diversity at its best, and I believe the same helped me see the hidden perspectives of everyday life.

I had the privilege of participating in exceptional technical conversations, expanding my knowledge and contributing to insightful discussions. Additionally, I was grateful for the opportunity to explore new parts of the world, fulfilling a dream and broadening my horizons. Montreal, a vibrant and multicultural city, captivated me with its rich history, diverse culture, and stunning architecture. From its charming neighborhoods to its bustling food scene (especially poutine!!), Montreal offers a unique blend of old-world charm and modern excitement.
